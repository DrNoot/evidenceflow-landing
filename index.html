There are several ways to approach screening. Each has strengths. Here is how they compare when the goal is speed, accuracy, and a defensible audit trail.

Manual Dual Review

  • Speed: ~25 papers/hour
  • Cost: $2,000-4,000+ per review
  • Automation: None
  • Timeline: Weeks to months

Rayyan

  • Speed: ~25 papers/hour
  • Cost: Free
  • Automation: Labels and filters

Good free tool for organizing screening. Still requires manual decisions on every paper.

ASReview

  • Speed: Variable
  • Cost: Free / open-source
  • Automation: Active learning
  • Timeline: Days to weeks

Strong active learning prioritization. Reduces workload but still requires a human decision on each paper.

  • Cost: $240+ per review
  • Automation: Workflow management
  • Timeline: Weeks to months

Excellent workflow management and conflict resolution. Does not automate the screening decisions themselves.

This is the right question to ask, and the short answer is: you should not trust it blindly, and the tool is not designed to be trusted blindly. EvidenceFlow is built as a second reviewer, not a replacement for your own judgment. You remain the primary screener. The AI acts as Reviewer #2, the same role a research assistant or colleague would fill.

The key safeguard is that the system is deliberately calibrated to err on the side of inclusion. When the AI is uncertain, it includes rather than excludes. This means you may see some false positives (papers included that probably should not be), but false negatives (the dangerous kind, where a relevant study gets missed) are minimized. Published research on LLM-assisted screening reports sensitivity (recall) of 99.5%, which exceeds the 92-95% typically observed in manual screening, particularly after reviewer fatigue sets in.

We recommend validating on a batch of papers you have already screened manually. Compare the AI's decisions with yours. If agreement is high (most teams see 95%+ concordance), you can proceed with confidence. If it is not, refine your protocol criteria before scaling up. Full transparency is essential and straightforward. EvidenceFlow generates a complete audit trail for every screening decision, including the AI model used, the reasoning for each decision, and confidence scores. This data can be reported directly in your methods section.

A suggested approach: "Title and abstract screening was performed independently by [Reviewer 1] and an AI-assisted screening tool (EvidenceFlow) using [model name]. The AI screening tool provided a documented rationale for each decision, which is available as supplementary material."

This framing is consistent with emerging best practices for reporting AI-assisted systematic reviews. The PRISMA-compliant export provides all the data you need for your flow diagram and supplementary materials.
